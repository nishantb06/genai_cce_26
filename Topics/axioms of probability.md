In our case, the three [[Axioms]] given above are those of Kolmogorov's probability. The Bayesian (or subjective) approach to probability relies on another set of axioms. We will not dive into the details of these historical issues; in this book, we will confine our discussion to the three axioms given above.

- Axiom I (Non-negativity) ensures that probability is never negative.
- Axiom II (Normalization) ensures that probability is never greater than 1.
- Axiom III (Additivity) allows us to add probabilities when two events do not overlap.
