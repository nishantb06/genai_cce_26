A slight variation of the [[Bernoulli Random Variable]] is the Rademacher random variable, which has two states: $+1$ and $-1$. The probability of getting $+1$ or $-1$ is each $1/2$. Therefore, the PMF of a Rademacher random variable is

$$p_X(-1) = \frac{1}{2}, \quad \text{and} \quad p_X(+1) = \frac{1}{2}.$$

if $X$ is a Rademacher random variable then $(X+1)/2 \sim \mathrm{Bernoulli}(1/2)$. Also show the converse: If $Y \sim \mathrm{Bernoulli}(1/2)$ then $2Y-1$ is a Rademacher random variable.
